Slots


ApplePi emulates the first 4 models of Apple II computers.  These models have seven expansion slotsnote1, numbered 1-7, which can hold peripheral controller cards.

Populated slots in the ApplePi:

Slot 1:    Printer card
Slot 3:    80-column card
Slot 6:    DiskII 5¼" floppy controller card
Slot 7:    A combination 3½" floppy / hard drive controller cardnote2

To use a slot, enter PR#n (i.e, "print to slot n") , where 'n' is a number from 1-7.

Slot 1

PR#1 Case 1: The 'Print to File' menu is not in effect:

By default, text output will be directed from the screen to the terminal at which ApplePi has been executed.  (If ApplePi was not executed from a terminal, text will simply be sent into the aether.)

PR#1 Case 2: 'Print Text' has been used to write text to a file:

Text will be written to the file; every byte will be flushed to disk as it is encountered.



Note1:
There was also a special 'slot 0', called the 'language card slot' on the AppleII and II+, or the 'auxiliary slot' on the IIe.  This was physically and electronically incompatible with the expansion slots, and used for 80-column display circuitry and/or memory expansion.  Its existence in ApplePi is currently (V0.2) a combination all the above.
